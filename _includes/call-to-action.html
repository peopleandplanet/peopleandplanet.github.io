About the project

The people and planet project is a growing archive of story maps or digital stories that combine maps, text and multimedia to define key concepts relating to nature, people and justice.

This is an educational tool developed by environmental justice scholars for anyone and anybody who wants to learn about equity-conscious environmentalism.

The project's name was inspired by Leah Thomas' "How to Dismantle Systems of Oppression to Protect People + Planet" We hope that as with Thomas' book, this archive can be used as a tool that "promotes awareness of the fundamental truth that we cannot save the planet without uplifting the voices of its people".

To utilize it, hover over the images below and click on key concepts to learn their definitions through story maps.
